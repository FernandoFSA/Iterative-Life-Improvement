## Step 2: Problem Assessment (10 minutes)

### Your Mission
Identify the specific problem to solve in your focus area.

### What To Do

   - What's not working?
   - What frustrates you most?
   - List 3-5 specific problems

2. **Rate each problem**:
   - Effort to fix (1=Very Easy, 2=Easy, 3=Medium, 4=Hard, 5=Very Hard)
   - Impact if solved (1=Very Low, 2=Low, 3=Medium, 4=High, 5=Very High)

## Step 3: Solution Assessment (15 minutes)

### Your Mission
Find the best solutions for your chosen problem.

### What To Do

1. **Understand the problem deeply**:
   - What exactly is wrong? _________________________________
   - What would "better" look like? _________________________

2. **Find the root cause** (ask "Why?" 3 times):
   - Why is this happening? _______________________________
   - Why is that happening? _______________________________  
   - Why is that happening? _______________________________

4. **Select using 80/20 rule**:
   - Add up all priority scores: Total = ___
   - Calculate 80%: ___ √ó 0.8 = ___
   - Pick top solutions that sum to 80% target

### Example (Irregular meals)
**Problem**: I eat at random times, often skip breakfast, rely on snacks
**Better**: Regular meal times, planned nutrition, consistent energy

**Root cause analysis**:
- Why irregular? ‚Üí No meal planning
- Why no planning? ‚Üí Don't know what to cook  
- Why don't know? ‚Üí Never learned meal prep basics

**Solutions**:

## Step 4: Planning and Execution (5 minutes)

### Your Mission
Turn your solutions into action.

### What To Do

1. **Set your target and deadline**:
   - Target: What specific outcome do you want?
   - Deadline: By when?

2. **Break into tasks**:
   - List specific actions for each solution
   - Identify what resources you need
   - Put in order (what first?)

3. **Create your control system**:
   - How will you measure progress?
   - When will you check progress?

### Example

**Target**: Eat regular, planned meals 6 days/week  
**Deadline**: 4 weeks from today

**Tasks this week**:
1. Download meal planning app (Sunday)
2. Research meal prep basics online (Sunday) 
3. Set 3 daily meal alarms (Sunday)
4. Plan next week's meals (Sunday)
5. Grocery shop with list (Sunday)
6. Prep 3 breakfasts and lunches (Sunday)

**Resources needed**: Phone, $50 grocery budget, 2 hours Sunday

**Progress tracking**: 
- Daily: Did I eat at alarm times? (yes/no)
- Weekly: How many planned meals did I eat? (goal: 18/21)
- Check-in: Every Sunday evening

## Troubleshooting

**"I have tied scores"**: Pick the one that feels most urgent to you right now

**"I can't think of 5 solutions"**: That's okay! Start with 3 and add more later

**"Everything seems important"**: Trust the numbers - they remove emotional bias

**"This feels overwhelming"**: Focus only on your highest-priority solution first
