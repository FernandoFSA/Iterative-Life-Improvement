# Multi-Cycle PDCA Example: Alex's 6-Month Journey

*A comprehensive case study showing multiple PDCA cycles and long-term improvement*

---

## Background

**Alex, 29, Software Developer**
- Works remotely, lives alone
- Feeling overwhelmed by multiple life areas needing improvement
- Wants systematic approach instead of random self-help attempts
- Commits to monthly PDCA cycles

---

| Life Area | Sub-Area Scores | Average |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| **Personal** | Self-knowledge(2), Being(1), Development(2), Leisure(3) | **2.0** ← Tied lowest |
| **Health** | Physical(1), Mental(2), Spiritual(2) | **1.67** ← Lowest! |
| **Relationships** | Family(3), Partner(1), Friends(2), Community(1) | **1.75** |
| **Career** | Finances(2), Development(3), Satisfaction(3) | **2.67** |

### Problem Assessment - Physical Health

| Problem | Effort | Impact | Priority |
|---------|--------|--------|----------|
| No sleep schedule | 2 | 5 | **2.5** ← Winner |
| Sedentary lifestyle | 4 | 4 | 1.0 |
| Poor nutrition | 3 | 4 | 1.33 |

## Key Methodology Insights

### What Made This Successful

1. **Systematic Selection Eliminates Overwhelm**
   - No more "everything needs fixing" paralysis
   - Clear priority at each cycle

2. **Quantitative Tracking Reveals Truth**  
   - 85% sleep success felt like failure until measured
   - Progress becomes undeniable with numbers

3. **Sequential Focus Builds Momentum**
   - Health success gave energy for relationship work
   - Stable foundations enabled career risk-taking

4. **80/20 Solutions Maximize ROI**
   - Environmental changes > willpower for habits
   - Scheduled connection > random relationship effort

### Common Challenges Overcome

- **"Everything needs improvement"** → Focus on lowest-scoring area first
- **"I don't know what to work on"** → Let the numbers decide  
- **"I keep failing at goals"** → Track systematically, adjust based on data
- **"Improvement feels impossible"** → Small consistent gains compound quickly

### Long-term Sustainability

**After 6 months, Alex has:**
- ✅ Systematic method for prioritizing life improvements
- ✅ Proven track record of achieving measurable results
- ✅ Multiple areas running on "autopilot" (stable routines)
- ✅ Confidence to tackle any new life challenge systematically

**The methodology became a life skill, not just a one-time improvement project.**
